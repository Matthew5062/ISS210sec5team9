Poverty Gap Amoung Major Racial and Ethnic Groups

Ethno-racial inequality in poverty is an enduring problem. It’s been found that Black, Latino, and Native Americans are significantly more likely to be in poverty than White and Asian individuals. Many try to explain that the poverty gap between races is due to behavior explanations, such as family structure. Now, researchers are trying to reorientate their explanations through structural, political, and critical race theories of poverty. One main structural explanation would be opportunities and constraints within economic contexts. An example of a political explanation is driven by power relations, institutions, and social policies. Finally, critical race theories of poverty emphasizes the systemic exclusion and institutional of racial minorities via discrimination. For example, Immigration laws are structural and political barriers that prevent people's access to labor markets and social policies.

Causes of Homelessness in the United States

The four major structural factors that cause homelessness are lack of low-cost housing, high poverty rates, poor economic conditions, and lack of community health care. The homelessness crisis in the United States cannot be fixed until these structural factors change. Mental health care needs to be more accessible. Low-cost housing must be available for even marginalized and disadvantaged members of society. There must be ample opportunities for stable, well-paid employment. Structural changes such as these must be made in order to help those facing homelessness.

Entreprenurial Development in Homesless and Unstably Housed Youth

A study was done regarding homelessness and unstably housed youth and their views on accessible entraprenurial development. The individuals in the study were interested in economic strengthening activities for various reasons such as not having a traditional income or education, the desire to be ones own boss, to find a way out of joblessness and illicit income, and to build on current entreprenurial activities. These youth also believe that entreprenurial interventions can improve their situational depression and help to promote productive behaviors. However, many individuals feel there are barriers between them and these entreprenurial activities such as lacking financial mentors, not knowing what is possible, having trouble changing one's prior mindset and fearing negatice reactions from peers. If we are able to find a way to comabt these barriers, we can help homeless and unstably housed youth to pursue a career in entreprenurialship and thus, decreasing homelessness and unstablely housed youth.
